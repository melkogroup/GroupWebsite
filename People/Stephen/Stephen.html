Stephen Inglis

About Stephen

Stephen started his graduate studies at Waterloo in 2008 after an undergraduate degree in physics at the University of British Columbia. His research has revolved around trying to explore and understand exotic phases, such as quantum spin liquids and critical systems. To this end he has used and developed techniques in classical and quantum Monte Carlo to explore condensed matter systems. His hope is that these new measures will give additional avenues for characterization of systems without clear signatures of order. Stephen also works on promoting novel visualization -- in his case the generation of images directly from Monte Carlo simulation. These sort of methods help captivate a larger audience from the physics and general community, and allow communication of often deeply mathematical concepts in a more palatable way. At the same time these methods retain the quantitative nature that is necessary in conveying results to the academic community without bias, an aspect that can often be lost when using simple diagrams or purely artistic interpretation to explain complex topics.
